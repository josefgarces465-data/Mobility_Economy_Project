# Urban Mobility & Economic Performance Analysis in Latin American Cities

## Overview

This project explores the relationship between urban traffic congestion and economic performance across major Latin American cities during 2024.

Using data from traffic and economic indicators, the analysis investigates whether higher congestion levels are associated with lower economic productivity and quality of life. The project combines data cleaning, transformation, integration, and exploratory data analysis (EDA) techniques to uncover meaningful patterns and support data-driven decision-making.

---

## Business Problem

Traffic congestion is one of the most significant challenges faced by growing urban areas. It impacts commuting times, operational efficiency, environmental sustainability, and overall economic productivity.

The objective of this analysis is to evaluate whether cities experiencing higher traffic congestion also show weaker economic indicators, helping identify opportunities for infrastructure improvements and urban planning initiatives.

---

## Data Sources

The analysis integrates information from:

- Traffic congestion datasets
- Economic performance indicators
- Urban mobility metrics

The datasets were merged and transformed to create a unified analytical view of mobility and economic activity across multiple cities.

## Project Workflow

### 1. Data Collection
- Imported mobility and economic datasets.
- Reviewed data structure and quality.

### 2. Data Cleaning
- Removed inconsistencies and missing values.
- Standardized column names and formats.
- Converted data types where necessary.

### 3. Data Transformation
- Filtered relevant observations.
- Aggregated information by city.
- Created analytical datasets for comparison.

### 4. Data Integration
- Combined mobility and economic datasets.
- Validated joins and data consistency.

### 5. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Evaluated congestion patterns across cities.
- Compared economic indicators between locations.
- Generated visualizations to identify trends and relationships.

### 6. Business Insights
- Assessed the relationship between congestion and economic performance.
- Identified cities with notable mobility challenges.
- Proposed areas where transportation investments could provide greater value.

---

## Key Findings

- Significant differences in congestion levels exist across Latin American cities.
- Some cities with lower congestion demonstrated stronger economic indicators.
- The relationship between congestion and economic performance is not always linear, suggesting that additional factors influence urban productivity.
- Certain cities emerged as potential candidates for transportation infrastructure improvements and mobility optimization initiatives.
